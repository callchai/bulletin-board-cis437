Faithful Poster,

Welcome to the Board


A place of expression, creativity, and community. Here, you can post things that will be seen by all who visit the Board. The Board is a place for everyone, and the Board's grace extends to all who enter.


Every few seconds, the board will update for any new posts.


You can view posts in full by clicking on them.


Posts with attachments, such as images, drawings or GIFs, may take a few seconds to load.



Posting

Click the Post button. Type your message, and click to post. You cannot edit once it is posted.


If your post is too long, it will appear truncated.


You cannot remove a post once it is submitted and inscribed on the board. If you wish to remove a post, you must wait for the board to reset.
If you regret the post you made, but have committed to posting, you cannot go back on your decision.


Drawings

In the post box, you can select draw to create a drawing post. You are given a set amount of space to draw.


Images and GIFs

You can also select to post an image or GIF. There are limits to the size and type of image you can post, so be sure to read the instructions in the post box carefully.


Scoring

Every post has a score in the bottom right. Clicking to view in full on a post will enable you to declare it as righteous or sinful. If your fellow posters deem your post as righteous, it will stand out. If it is deemed sinful, you will be put on trial.


Denouncement

Posts from authors who have been banished or exiled will be marked as denounced. The contents of the post and the author will be erased.

Your Identity

The Board's divinity grants you a new identity upon the board to reinvent yourself on your first visit. If you return, you will keep your identity.


You may think your new identity and anonymity is a license to sin, but remember the commandments you agreed to.


Search by name

Use the search bar to find posts by a specific poster's name. Posts from the specified author will be highlighted. Posts from other authors will be dimmed. Clear the search to view all posts in their full glory.

Board Resets

To maintain the sanctity of the Board and prevent it from being overrun by blasphemous posts, the Board naturally resets on a timely schedule. The lifespan clock shows how long the current board has been alive. When it resets, all posts are cleared and the clock starts anew cycle.


The Board is currently set to reset every 24 hours at midnight-EDT.


Resetting the board reminds us of the impermanence of all things. Not only that, it encourages us to post with intention and purpose, for we know that our posts will not last forever.


It also keeps the Serpent at bay (bills from Google), for each reset is like a sacrifice that prevents the Serpent from demanding a payment.



Judgment

Should a poster commit a most grievous sin against the Board, they shall condemn all to its wrath, and from that judgment shall arise a cleansing flood, purging every poster and post from the Board, and restarting the clock to its origin.

These are the terms you accepted upon your entrance to the board. Remember the oath you took, and the commandments you agreed to. While the Board's grace extends its hand to all, it still demands respect and adherence to its rules.

Trials

If a posts is deemed too sinful by the people, the author of the sinful posts will be put on trial.


Trial Start

A trial begins, and everyone currently on the Board will be notified. The accused transgressor will be able to give reasoning to defend themselves.


Jury Duty

Once the defendant has given their defense, everyone will be invited to cast Judgment on the transgressor, and decide the fate of the defendant. There are three different verdicts:

  • Forgive: forgive the transgressor, and let them stay on the Board.
  • Banish: condemn the transgressor, and cast them aside; preventing their return, and eliminating all heresy they have posted.
  • Exile: If there is a tie, the Board will exile the accused. Casting them out for a limited time, and rendering all their posts as heresy. After their penance, they will be welcomed back.
